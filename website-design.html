The Challenge

[StarTea Products:
Tea & Supplements]

How do you translate a wellness brand into a digital experience that feels both modern and timeless? StarTea needed an online presence that would capture the essence of mindful living—calm yet engaging, professional yet approachable—while appealing to a wellness-conscious audience ages 18-40.

The design challenge centered on creating a cohesive visual language that would communicate trust and relaxation simultaneously. Every design decision needed to serve both aesthetic beauty and functional clarity, ensuring users could easily discover products while feeling immersed in StarTea's wellness philosophy.

[Initial Sketches:
Early Concepts]

Working alongside Isabella Alfonso (Lead Designer) and David Etherage, I focused on user research insights, content strategy, accessibility compliance, and ensuring the information architecture supported intuitive navigation across Homepage, About, Shop/Products, Wellness Blog, and Contact pages.

Research & Strategy

[Competitive Analysis:
Industry Patterns]

We began with competitive analysis of five wellness brand websites, identifying successful patterns and missed opportunities. Most competitors leaned heavily into either clinical minimalism or overly ornate aesthetics—we saw space for something that balanced polish with warmth, sophistication with accessibility.

Our user research with six participants revealed critical insights that shaped every design decision. Wellness shoppers want transparent ingredient information presented clearly, calming color palettes that don't sacrifice readability, and mobile-first experiences that feel just as considered as desktop. They value brands that communicate both expertise and approachability.

[User Personas:
Target Audience]

From these conversations, I developed personas representing our core user segments and a content strategy that would speak directly to their needs. The tone guide I created—relaxing, mindful, youthful but professional—became our north star, influencing not just copy but visual rhythm, spacing decisions, and interaction patterns.

[Wireframes:
Information Architecture]

Wireframing focused on clear information hierarchy and intuitive user flows. We mapped customer journeys from discovery through potential purchase, ensuring each page transition felt natural and purposeful. Early wireframes tested navigation patterns and content organization before investing in visual design.

Design System

[Color Palette:
Wellness Brand Colors]

Isabella developed a sophisticated design system grounded in wellness aesthetics—soft greens evoking natural ingredients, warm neutrals suggesting handcrafted care, strategic pops of gold for premium touches. Every color choice was validated against WCAG AA standards, ensuring beauty never compromised accessibility.

Typography paired a refined serif for headings (conveying timeless elegance) with a clean sans-serif for body text (ensuring effortless readability). The type scale established clear visual hierarchy while maintaining generous line height and comfortable font sizes across all breakpoints.

[Component Library:
Buttons & Cards]

Our component library standardized buttons, cards, form inputs, and navigation elements—creating consistency while allowing flexibility. Rounded corners softened the interface without feeling juvenile. Subtle shadows added depth without cluttering. Every micro-interaction was considered for both delight and clarity.

[Accessibility Testing:
WCAG Compliance]

I led accessibility compliance checks throughout the design process, not as final validation but as ongoing design criteria. Color contrast ratios, keyboard navigation patterns, screen reader compatibility, focus states—these weren't constraints but opportunities to make the design work better for everyone.

Final Design

[Homepage:
Hero Section]

The homepage welcomes visitors with carefully composed product photography, inviting them into StarTea's world of mindful wellness. Featured products are showcased with breathing room—enough visual interest to engage without overwhelming. Copy introduces the brand story concisely, encouraging deeper exploration.

Product pages balance detailed information with scannable layouts. Ingredient lists, brewing instructions, and wellness benefits are organized hierarchically, letting different user types find what matters most to them. Product imagery emphasizes both the packaging design and the experience of use.

[Shop Page:
Product Grid]

The Wellness Blog extends StarTea's expertise beyond products, positioning the brand as a trusted resource for holistic wellness. Article cards use imagery and typography to convey content themes at a glance, while maintaining the calming aesthetic users expect from the brand.

[Mobile Views:
Responsive Design]

Usability testing with six participants validated our design decisions while revealing opportunities for refinement. Users praised the calming aesthetic and intuitive navigation, requesting only minor adjustments to button sizing and product filtering. Post-testing iterations focused on these details, ensuring the final prototype felt polished and production-ready.

[Instagram Mockups:
Social Extensions]

As a stretch goal, we extended the design system into social media mockups—demonstrating how StarTea's visual language could translate across platforms. These mockups proved the design system's flexibility and helped complete the brand's cohesive digital presence.
