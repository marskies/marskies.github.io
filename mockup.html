Learn To Leap

A learning platform that meets new instructors where they are — and gets out of their way once they’re moving.
Brief Research System Outcome Reflection
02 / 05
Chapter 02 · Research

What we found

[ Chapter image · swap per tab ]

Interviews with twelve instructors across four studios revealed a pattern: the best mentors taught through a shared vocabulary the platforms didn’t support. We designed around that vocabulary instead of around the LMS.

Three themes surfaced again and again. First, instructors wanted scaffolding for the first six months and autonomy after. Platforms forced them into the opposite shape. Second, the most experienced mentors taught through quick verbal cues and physical demonstrations — both lost in text-heavy lesson templates. Third, retention was tied less to compensation and more to whether instructors felt their craft was respected by the tool they used every day.

We translated those themes into design principles before touching a single screen. Scaffolding-then-autonomy became progressive disclosure. Verbal cues became audio annotations attached to lesson steps. Respect for craft became a quiet refusal to hide what advanced instructors already knew, even from beginners. The full research deck contains twelve interview synthesis maps, three competitive teardowns, and a usability findings reel from the second pilot.

The synthesis took longer than the design. Six weeks of interviews compressed into four weeks of clustering, theming, and testing those themes back with the same instructors before any visual work began. That investment is what made the rest of the project move quickly — we never had to second-guess what we were building or why.
